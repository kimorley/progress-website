Welcome

The PROGgnosis RESearch Strategy (PROGRESS) Partnership is a UK Medical Research Council (MRC) funded, international, interdisciplinary collaboration developing understanding in research into quality of care outcomes, prognostic factors, risk prediction models, and predictors of differential treatment response.

The objectives of the Partnership are:

to critically develop concepts, methods and recommendations for improving prognosis research, and systematically apply these across different disease areas, in order to enhance the translational impact of prognosis research;
bring together leaders in different clinical disciplines for novel collaborative opportunities;
to develop guidelines, workshops and a prognosis research training courses.

The PROGRESS Partnership is led by investigators from six UK academic institutions (for more information, see the People page):

Prof. Harry Hemingway (UCL) Prof. Aroon Hingorani (UCL) Dr. Pablo Perel (LSHTM)
Prof. Ian Roberts (LSHTM) Prof. Peter Croft (Keele University) Prof. Danielle van der Windt (Keele University)
Dr. Richard Riley (Birmingham University) Prof. Doug Altman (University of Oxford) Prof. Adam Timmis (QMUL)

International Summer School, 2013

The PROGRESS partnership is hosting a three-day International Summer School in Prognosis Research from the 26th to 28th of June, 2013. It is designed to introduce the key components and uses of prognosis research to health professionals and researchers.
